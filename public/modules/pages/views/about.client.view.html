This is an early prototype of a travellers' community for sharing, hosting and getting people together. Built with hitchhikers in mind.

Travel anywhere and easily find great people who want to meet you as well. See where other travellers are and help each other out, whether through welcoming them to your home, sharing your stories or becoming friends.

Ideas

Other social networks

By connecting to other social networks (e.g. Facebook), you can see where all your friends are on the map, what you have in common and show others who you are even without filling your profile or having many references.

This is still a feature in planning.

The core team

Trustroots is being built by a small team of open-source developers and free economy activists who felt that the world of sharing is being taken over by corporations trying to monetize people's willingness to help each other. We are long time hospitality exchange enthusiasts with backgrounds in some other notable projects.

Communities

Every site user will belong to one or more communities. For example, you could join the "hitchhiker", "geek", “improvisational music”, and “vegan” communities. You might decide to be visible to the members of all of them – or only one or two where you feel you best belong.

Trustroots is being built at first with hitchhikers in mind. We will expand later for other target groups. When travelling by hitchhiking, searching for a host in the hitching community makes sense. Another hitchhiker will better understand a traveller not knowing the exact date of arrival or showing up in the middle of the night.

This is still a feature in planning.

Volunteering

We appreciate a hard working, getting things done attitude. And we love great ideas. We'll aim to keep the entry level for volunteers as low as possible. No bureaucracy. Pragmatic approach. Trust.

Right now structures for volunteering are still getting underway, but you can simply drop us a line and tell us about your ideas! If you're a developer, check GitHub.

Free and open source forever

You can trust us. This is not some venture capital-funded startup. We've been running other free and open projects for a decade now and we hope our deeds so far speak for us.

Transparency & legal

We will form a legal organization behind Trustroots and your help with this is very welcome.

Geeky stuff

We're huge fans of distributed networks, self hosted apps, API first approach etc. While it might not be the time to concentrate on this stuff just yet, it certainly gets us excited thinking about all the possibilities.
